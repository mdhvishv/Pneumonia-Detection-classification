Difference in lungs

Normal lungs have a clear and transparent appearance on imaging and do not exhibit signs of infection or inflammation. In contrast, lungs affected by pneumonia often show opacities or areas of increased density on imaging due to infection, inflammation, and fluid accumulation. Symptoms associated with pneumonia, such as respiratory distress and fever, are absent in individuals with normal lungs. Recognizing these differences is crucial for accurate diagnosis and treatment of pneumonia.The differences between normal and pneumonia-affected lungs is essential.
